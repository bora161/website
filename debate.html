The Debate Stage
The debate stage is where delegates formally discuss, argue, and defend their positions on draft resolutions. It is the heart of the Model United Nations committee session, where policies are scrutinized, amendments are proposed, and consensus is built through structured dialogue. Understanding the debate stage is crucial for effective participation and successful resolution passage.
Formal Debate
Delegates speak according to the speakers' list, presenting arguments and counterarguments.
Moderated Caucus
Short, structured discussion on a specific subtopic guided by the chair.
Unmoderated Caucus
Delegates move freely to negotiate, lobby, and draft amendments in an informal setting.
Points of Order
Used to raise procedural issues or clarify rules during debate.
Amendment Proposal
Delegates formally suggest changes to clauses in draft resolutions.
Yielding the Floor
Delegates can yield remaining speaking time to another delegate or the chair.
