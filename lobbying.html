Lobbying in Model United Nations
Lobbying is the process where delegates negotiate, persuade, and build support for clauses within draft resolutions before formal voting. Effective lobbying ensures that delegates can form coalitions, incorporate key amendments, and maximize the chances of their proposals being accepted.
Informal Negotiation
Delegates discuss potential resolutions and amendments privately to gain support.
Coalition Building
Forming groups of delegates with shared interests to strengthen voting power.
Draft Amendments
Proposing changes to draft resolutions to reflect lobbying agreements.
Sponsorship
Supporting or officially backing a draft resolution or clause.
